Kepler-22b
Kepler-22b is one of the most intriguing exoplanets discovered by NASA’s Kepler Space Telescope. It was the first exoplanet confirmed to be in the habitable zone of a star similar to our Sun, making it a major milestone in the search for Earth-like planets. However, with limited detailed knowledge about its atmosphere or surface, scientists are cautious about speculating on whether it could actually support life.

Proxima Centauri b
Proxima Centauri b is an exoplanet orbiting the star Proxima Centauri, which is the closest known star to the Sun, located about 4.24 light-years away in the constellation Centaurus. Proxima Centauri b is a fascinating exoplanet due to its proximity, potential habitability, and the challenges it poses to life. Its discovery has sparked significant interest in studying the closest star system to our own.

HD 209458 b, also known as Osiris, is a well-studied exoplanet located approximately 159 light-years away in the Pegasus constellation. HD 209458 b
HD 209458 b, also known as Osiris, is a well-studied exoplanet located approximately 159 light-years away in the Pegasus constellation. It was one of the first exoplanets to be observed using the transit method, where the planet passes in front of its host star, causing a slight dimming of the star's light. This exoplanet remains a vital object for ongoing research, offering insights into the dynamics of gas giants, atmospheric loss, and the behavior of planets that orbit close to their parent stars.
